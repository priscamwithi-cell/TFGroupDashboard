Management Summary

A quick-reference brief for leadership review and approval

Vision 2029
By 2029, Tearfund will be known as a mission-driven employer of choice — attracting diverse, locally rooted talent, developing people to lead, and creating the conditions for people to thrive and stay — in direct service of the goal of reaching 250,000 churches by 2030.

What is this?

A four-year integrated strategy covering how Tearfund plans, acquires, manages and develops its people — consolidating the Workforce Capability Strategy and Internal Mobility Strategy into a single framework, directly aligned to the 250,000 churches vision.

Why now?

Tearfund is shifting from a project implementer to a facilitator and influencer. This requires a fundamentally different workforce — more locally led, more technically specialised, more capable of enabling others. The three most urgent gaps are: talent acquisition pipelines that match our evolving model; management systems that develop and retain people; and localisation of leadership to 70% non-UK by 2028.

Strategic Context

The transformation imperative, key drivers and external trends

The Transformation Imperative

Tearfund's programme strategy represents a fundamental shift in how we work. Moving from a project implementer to a facilitator and influencer requires different capabilities, mindsets, and ways of working. This talent strategy exists to ensure our people are equipped, enabled, and positioned to lead that transformation — in direct service of the goal of serving and resourcing 250,000 churches by 2030.

Our Talent Philosophy

The values and principles that guide how we treat our people

Mission Alignment

Every element of this strategy exists to serve Tearfund's mission: to see people freed from poverty, living transformed lives and reaching their God-given potential.

Guiding Principles

Mission-led

Talent decisions are made in service of Tearfund's mission. We hire for values alignment alongside technical competence.

Locally Rooted

We prioritise building talent from within the communities and countries where we work. Localisation is not a target — it is a way of operating.

Fair and Inclusive

We design fair, transparent processes that give all staff equal access to opportunities, development and recognition.

Evidence-Informed

We use data to understand our workforce, identify risk, and make better decisions — from workforce planning to succession to learning investment.

Growth-Oriented

We invest in developing people at every level. Career progression, internal mobility and learning are rights, not privileges.

Sustainable

We build talent practices that are affordable, scalable and durable — not dependent on large one-off investments.

Pillar 1 — Workforce Planning

Building the workforce Tearfund needs, now and in the future

Strategic Intent

Tearfund will move from reactive headcount management to strategic workforce planning — anticipating skills needs, modelling scenarios, and ensuring the right people are in the right roles to deliver mission.

Current State

  • No consistent organisation-wide workforce planning methodology currently in use
  • Contract type distribution varies significantly across groups with limited central visibility
  • Localisation tracking exists at headcount level but lacks role-level granularity or targets
  • Succession planning is informal and undocumented for the majority of critical roles

Strategic Objectives

  • Establish a consistent, annually updated workforce plan for each group and region by 2027
  • Develop a Critical Roles Framework identifying positions essential to mission delivery
  • Build scenario models for workforce composition under different funding and programme footprint assumptions
  • Set and track localisation targets at role level across all 45 countries
  • Integrate workforce planning into the annual business planning cycle by end of 2026

Pillar 3 — Talent Management

Recognising, retaining and mobilising our best people

Strategic Intent

Tearfund will build transparent, evidence-based talent management practices — identifying high-potential staff, managing performance fairly, enabling internal mobility, and reducing avoidable attrition.

Five Internal Mobility Gaps

Gap AreaDescription
Talent Data VisibilityNo skills matrices, performance tracking, or mobility trend analysis. Staff and managers lack data to make informed internal movement decisions.
Career Opportunity VisibilityNo centralised platform for internal positions, projects, or learning opportunities. Engagement findings confirm staff have limited awareness of available opportunities.
Career Development ConversationsManagers are not consistently holding career development conversations — a key driver of retention risk.
Performance DifferentiationNo framework for distinguishing performance levels or linking performance data to development and reward decisions.
Internal Mobility CultureCredential-focused rather than skills-first; talent hoarding by managers; rejected internal candidates are twice as likely to leave the organisation.

Seven Strategic Interventions

1

Skills-First Internal Mobility Framework

Move from credential-based to competency-based internal matching. Build a skills taxonomy for core job families and use it to surface internal candidates before external advertising begins.

Owner: Talent Acquisition Lead / P&C Timeline: Q3 2026
2

Internal Talent Marketplace

Establish a centrally visible platform for all internal vacancies, project secondments, and short-term assignments. Equitable field country access essential.

Owner: P&C / IT Timeline: Q2 2026
3

Structured Career Development Conversations

Mandatory career development conversation framework for all line managers — at minimum annually, linked to performance review.

Owner: L&D / HRBPs Timeline: Q3 2026
4

Manager Capability for Mobility

Address talent hoarding through training on shared ownership of talent. Incentivise managers who develop and release staff.

Owner: L&D / HRBPs Timeline: Q4 2026
5

Rejected Internal Candidate Protocol

Structured feedback and support for unsuccessful internal candidates. Evidence shows rejected internal candidates are twice as likely to leave — a critical retention intervention.

Owner: HRBPs / Talent Lead Timeline: Q3 2026
6

Short-Term Assignments & Secondments

Formalised short-term assignment and secondment programme enabling cross-functional and cross-regional experience.

Owner: Talent Lead / Country Directors Timeline: Q1 2027
7

Internal Mobility Metrics & Reporting

Establish baseline metrics for internal mobility rate, internal fill rate, time-to-transition, and retention of internal movers. Report quarterly to P&C leadership.

Pillar 4 — Talent Development

Building organisational capability across five domains

Strategic Intent

Tearfund will build a learning and development culture that is equitable, mission-focused and practically accessible for staff in every country and context. Development is structured across five capability domains.

Five Development Domains

Domain 1

Influencing and Facilitation Capabilities

The most critical capability gap — shifting from implementer to influencer. Building facilitative leadership, advocacy enablement, partnership management and cultural intelligence.

Domain 2

Integrated and Thematic Expertise

Deepening technical excellence in CCT, EES, RPS and C2R through structured learning pathways, communities of practice, secondments and annual thematic symposiums.

Domain 3

Data-Driven Decision Making & GIF

Embedding GIF capability across the organisation. 100% of programme staff GIF-capable by Year 2 (end 2027). Data literacy programme with tiered paths by role level.

Domain 4

Leadership and Management Excellence

Regional Leadership Academy (3 cohorts × 20 participants; executive coaching for 30 leaders; 50 stretch assignments). Change leadership workshops, 360-degree feedback, Mentoring Circles.

Domain 5

Digital and Operational Agility

Digital literacy, mobile-first LMS deployment (low-bandwidth field contexts), technology adoption support, agile methodology workshops, virtual collaboration skills.

Key Named Programmes

Regional Leadership Academy

3 cohorts × 20 participants. Executive coaching for 30 leaders. 50 stretch assignments. Accelerates local leadership pipeline.

Esther Initiative

Women's accelerator programme with executive coaching cohorts. Target: 45% women in leadership. Launches Q2 2027.

Localisation Development Track

Targeted investment in high-potential national staff identified for leadership roles. Supports the 70% non-UK staff target by 2028.

Enablers & Cross-Cutting Themes

The lenses applied consistently across all talent activity

How to read this section

These are not separate workstreams. They are design requirements applied to every action, every process and every decision across all four pillars.

1. Localisation

Every element of this strategy is designed to accelerate the shift toward a locally led workforce. Role-level localisation targets, skills-first hiring, the Localisation Development Track, and national staff representation in succession plans are non-negotiable design requirements across all four pillars.

2. Diversity, Equity & Inclusion

DEI is embedded across the full talent lifecycle. Annual equity audits of promotion, performance and pay decisions apply. DEI data disaggregated by gender, nationality and region actively informs decisions in every pillar.

3. Budget Sustainability

This strategy is designed to be delivered within constrained resources. Exhaust internal talent before recruiting externally. Favour peer learning and manager-led development over externally commissioned programmes.

Glossary of Key Terms

Definitions of terminology used throughout this strategy document

How to use this glossary

Written in plain language for leadership and management audiences encountering HR or strategy terminology for the first time.

Tearfund-Specific Terms

CCT
Church and Community Transformation — one of Tearfund's four corporate priority areas.
EES
Economic and Environmental Sustainability — covers livelihoods, food security and environmental stewardship programmes.
RPS
Resilience and Peacebuilding — covers disaster risk management, conflict resolution and community resilience.
C2R
Church to Response — enables churches to respond rapidly and effectively in humanitarian crises.
GIF
Global Impact Framework — Tearfund's organisation-wide system for measuring and reporting programme impact.
Operating Models 1/2/3
Model 1 (Influencing Only), Model 2 (Influencing + Resourcing), Model 3 (Full Engagement).
HRBP
HR Business Partner — a P&C professional embedded with a specific group or region.
PTB Fellows
A 2-year rotational graduate programme targeting Model 3 countries. Builds national staff pipeline at entry level.
Esther Initiative
Tearfund's women's accelerator programme. Target: 45% women in leadership by 2028.
Homecoming Initiative
Diaspora recruitment campaign targeting African and Asian professionals living abroad.
FTC
Fixed-Term Contract — an employment contract with a defined end date.
PPP
Purchasing Power Parity — adjusting salaries to account for different costs of living across countries.

HR / Talent Strategy Terms

Talent Acquisition
A strategic, long-term approach to identifying and building pipelines of future talent — broader than recruitment.
Internal Mobility
Movement of employees into different roles within the organisation — promotions, lateral moves, secondments.
9-Box Talent Review
A framework assessing employees on current performance and future potential to identify high-potential staff.
Career Lattice
A modern alternative to the career ladder — staff move sideways, diagonally or across functions, not just upward.
Skills-First Hiring
Removing credential requirements and assessing candidates purely on demonstrated capability.
Succession Planning
Identifying and preparing potential future holders of key leadership and critical roles.
EVP
Employer Value Proposition — the full package of reasons why someone would choose to work for Tearfund.
DEI
Diversity, Equity and Inclusion — ensuring the workforce reflects the communities Tearfund serves.
Localisation
The deliberate shift of decision-making and leadership toward national and regional staff by 2028.
LMS
Learning Management System — digital platform for delivering, tracking and managing staff learning.
Talent Hoarding
When managers resist releasing high-performing staff for internal moves or development opportunities elsewhere.
PDP
Personal Development Plan — a structured document setting out learning goals and career aspirations.
